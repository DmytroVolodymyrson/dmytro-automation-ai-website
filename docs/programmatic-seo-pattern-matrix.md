# Programmatic SEO Pattern Matrix

Last updated: 2026-04-17

## Bottom line

Yes, we can generate hundreds of articles and decision pages technically.

For **dmytroai.com**, we should **not** do a blind 100-500 page rollout yet.
The right move is a **bounded 20-30 page pilot**, then scale only after:
- pages index cleanly
- content quality stays high
- GEO citations begin to move
- booked-call / CTA data shows signal

## What we should scale

### Pattern 1 — Geo service pages
**Why:** closest to the video's “service in city” pattern, but still commercially real for this site.

Good examples:
- Calgary
- Edmonton
- Alberta
- Canada

Quality rule:
- no fake city explosion
- only pages that are believable for a remote consultant serving North America

## Pattern 2 — Industry pages with proof or adjacent proof
**Why:** strongest mix of SEO intent + GEO extractability.

Good examples:
- HVAC
- Restaurants
- Real estate
- Mortgage brokers
- Dental / clinics
- Law firms

Quality rule:
- must reference a real case study, adjacent operational proof, or repeatable service pattern
- no invented niche expertise

## Pattern 3 — Problem / job-to-be-done pages
**Why:** maps tightly to buyer questions and AEO/GEO answer extraction.

Good examples:
- after-hours lead capture
- missed-call follow-up automation
- booking confirmation and reminder automation
- database reactivation
- AI phone answering for service businesses

Quality rule:
- must answer the problem in the first 150 words
- must include implementation considerations and CTA context

## Pattern 4 — Comparison / decision pages
**Why:** already working well for the site structure and highly compatible with AI answer engines.

Good examples:
- consultant vs agency
- chatbot vs voice agent
- n8n vs Zapier
- GoHighLevel vs custom CRM
- appointment setter vs human staff / VA

Quality rule:
- must contain an actual decision framework, not generic listicle copy

## What we should NOT scale yet

- hundreds of city pages
- generic top-of-funnel “what is AI” articles
- pages for industries with no proof, no angle, and no case-study adjacency
- calculator / tool pages without strong surrounding static content

## Publish gate for every page

A page is publishable only if it has:
- clear direct answer in the opening
- unique H1/title/description
- page-specific FAQ
- at least one relevant case-study or proof reference
- “good fit / not fit” or equivalent filtering section
- internal links to related pages and booking CTA
- schema markup appropriate to the page
- `llm.txt` / `llms.txt` update if it adds a new service/topic cluster

## Scale decision

Scale from 30 pages to 100+ only if:
- at least 20 pilot pages are indexed
- no major thin-content / cannibalization issues appear
- GEO tracking shows non-zero movement on at least some pages
- pages generate either qualified traffic, CTA clicks, or booked calls

If those signals do not appear, improve the pilot instead of multiplying pages.
