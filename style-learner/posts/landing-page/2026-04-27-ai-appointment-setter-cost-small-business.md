Heading: AI Appointment Setter Cost for Small Business
Body: If you are pricing AI appointment setters, the useful question is not "what is the cheapest monthly tool?" It is "what kind of booking workflow am I actually trying to automate?" A small business that only needs fast web-lead scheduling should not buy the same stack as a business that needs voice intake, qualification, calendar rules, reminders, CRM routing, and human fallback for edge cases. This page breaks down realistic appointment-setter pricing specifically — not broad AI automation budgets, and not generic software listicles — so you can budget the narrowest build that actually protects revenue.
Sub: Below: what different appointment-setter builds usually cost, what pushes the price up, when a simpler setup is enough, and how to sanity-check the ROI before you overbuy.

### New pricing-breakdown section
Heading: What each pricing band actually includes
Intro: The table above compresses a lot of scope variation into a few rows. Here is what you are actually paying for at each level, so you can match the band to your workflow instead of guessing.

1. "$1.2K–$2.2K build — basic web-form or SMS booking"
- Explains one-channel inbound booking, single-calendar slot offering, one reminder, no voice handling, and lightweight CRM record creation.
- Frames this as the fit when the leak is slow manual response to straightforward online inquiries.

2. "$1.8K–$3.2K build — calendar-linked setter with reminders"
- Explains buffer rules, appointment-type routing, working-hours enforcement, reschedules/cancellations, and multi-step reminders.
- Frames this as the common first build when the business already has a CRM and needs booking rules to behave cleanly.

3. "$2.5K–$4.5K build — qualification + CRM handoff"
- Explains service-area/job-type/budget/urgency screening before booking.
- Explains qualified-vs-unqualified logic, CRM routing tags, and human-handoff triggers.
- Reframes this band as a revenue filter, not just a scheduler.

4. "$3K–$5.5K build — voice-based inbound appointment setter"
- Explains live-call answering, real-time conversation handling, interruptions, off-script recovery, hold/transfer logic, and telephony usage costs.
- Frames this as the fit when inbound calls are the main lead channel.

5. "$2.5K–$4.8K build — after-hours booking + missed-call recovery"
- Explains text-back / voicemail-triggered SMS / web-chat recovery and warm callback capture.
- Ties this band to nights/weekends as the lost-opportunity window.

6. "$4.5K–$7K+ build — multi-location or dispatch-aware booking"
- Explains location/territory/team routing, per-location calendar rules, and multiplied fallback logic.
- Frames this as only relevant for businesses with real dispatch or multi-location complexity.
