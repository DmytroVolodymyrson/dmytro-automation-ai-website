Heading: Review Request Automation ROI for Small Business
Body: Review request automation usually earns its keep in a small service business when completed jobs are happening consistently, public proof still depends too much on memory, and the business can recover even a modest number of additional quality reviews each month without pushing unhappy customers into a public ask too early. The real ROI is not just "more reviews." It is the combined value of cleaner local-trust signals, fewer missed asks after finished work, less office time spent remembering who should get the next message, and better protection against sending a review request before a complaint or unresolved issue gets routed back inside. The right payback test stays conservative: estimate how many completed jobs never get a real review ask today, assume only a modest lift in review volume, add back the manual follow-up time the owner or office staff keeps spending, and check whether the workflow still pays for itself without heroic assumptions. This page is for that exact question: whether a focused post-job review workflow is financially worth building now, when a narrower first version is enough, and when the math is still too weak to justify the project.
