## Final Published Text

### Hero
Heading: Estimate Follow-Up Setup: Hire Help or DIY?
Body: DIY can be completely reasonable when estimate follow-up is still a narrow build: one clear estimate-sent trigger, one owner, one or two reminder steps, and low enough estimate volume that mistakes will not quietly cost real jobs. Hiring help usually becomes the better move once estimates matter operationally across the field and the office, the CRM has to stay trustworthy, stale-estimate logic actually changes who should act next, and live replies need to route back to the right human fast. The real question is not whether you could build it yourself. It is whether this estimate-stage workflow is important enough that you want it live, reliable, and revenue-safe without burning nights on trigger cleanup, estimator handoff, stop rules, and revision-request routing.
Sub: Below: when DIY estimate-follow-up setup is still a smart move, when expert help usually pays for itself, what each path really costs, and how to tell whether you need a tutorial, a bounded setup engagement, or a simpler estimate workflow first.
