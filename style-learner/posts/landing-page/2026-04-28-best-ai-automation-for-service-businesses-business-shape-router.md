## Final Published Text

### Section heading
Pick your first automation by how your business actually runs

### Intro copy
Budget matters, but so does operating model. An owner-operator on a job site all day has a different first-automation need than a business with a front-desk dispatcher or a salesperson nurturing long-cycle deals. Find your shape below.

### Router cards
- **Owner-operator (you're on the job site all day)**
  - Biggest leak: missed calls and form submissions that go unanswered while you're working.
  - Recommended first automation: missed-call text-back paired with instant form response.
  - Named proof: Paris Cafe recovered 100% of after-hours calls.
- **Dispatcher / admin-heavy (front desk handles intake)**
  - Biggest leak: scheduling, intake, and after-hours voicemail drop-off while staff juggle calls and admin.
  - Recommended first automation: AI booking agent / phone-answering setup.
- **Long sales cycle / follow-up heavy (deals take weeks or months)**
  - Biggest leak: quotes and leads die in the follow-up gap because nobody has time to chase every lead for 2–4 weeks.
  - Recommended first automation: multi-touch SMS + email follow-up sequences.
  - Named proof: WheelsFeels recovered a 185% conversion lift across 5,600+ leads.
- **High repeat / recurring service (your database is your biggest asset)**
  - Biggest leak: past customers and aged leads sit untouched in the CRM.
  - Recommended first automation: database reactivation campaign.
