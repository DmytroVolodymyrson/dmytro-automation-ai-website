### Three ways to get AI automation into your business
Intro: Each option has genuine strengths. The right choice depends on your budget, timeline, and how much hands-on attention your automation needs:
- Solo consultant: One person who diagnoses your workflow, builds the system, and hands it off. Direct access to the builder. Fast turnaround. Best for businesses that want a working system without the overhead of a large team.
- Agency: A team with account managers, project managers, and specialists. Good for large, multi-discipline projects that need design, marketing, and automation all at once. Higher cost, longer timelines, more process.
- DIY (Zapier, Make, ChatGPT): You build it yourself using no-code tools. Low upfront cost, full control. Works well for simple, single-step automations. Gets difficult fast when you need multi-step logic, error handling, or custom integrations.

### When each option is the right call
Three-column breakdown with specific criteria for each path. Consultant: specific workflow, direct access, $2K–$8K budget, speed. Agency: multi-discipline, $10K+ budget, formal procurement. DIY: single-step automations, experimenting, tight budget.

### The costs nobody talks about
- Agency overhead: Paying for account managers, project managers, office space. The builder might represent 30% of the bill.
- DIY time cost: At $100/hour of your time, a 40-hour DIY project costs $4,000 in opportunity cost alone.
- Reliability cost: Broken automations that drop leads cost real revenue. DIY breaks more, agencies use generic templates.
- Iteration speed: Consultant turns changes in days. Agency: ticket queue. DIY: back to debugging.

### What consultant-built automation looks like
Case studies: Paris Cafe voice agent (100% after-hours calls), Instagram lead generation (50+ leads/day at $0.29), E-commerce CRM (3x follow-up capacity). Framed as "one person, one project" results.

### How to evaluate an AI automation consultant
Green flags: Shows built systems, asks about workflow first, fixed pricing, direct builder access, upfront about non-fit, you own everything.
Red flags: Promises before understanding, vague pricing, can't talk to builder, proprietary lock-in, no demos, no exit plan.
