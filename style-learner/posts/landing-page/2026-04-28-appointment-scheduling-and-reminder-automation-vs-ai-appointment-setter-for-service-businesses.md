### Hero
Heading: Appointment Scheduling & Reminder Automation vs. AI Appointment Setter for Service Businesses
Body: A lot of service businesses use these two ideas interchangeably even though they fix different leaks. An AI appointment setter helps at the front of the workflow: answering new opportunities, qualifying, routing, and helping get the booking moving in the first place. Appointment scheduling and reminder automation helps further downstream: confirmations, reminders, prep steps, reschedules, and protecting booked visits after interest already exists. If you confuse the two, you can overbuild the wrong layer. Some businesses do not need a front-end AI appointment setter yet. They already have enough real booking opportunities and the real leak now sits in preventable calendar breakdowns. Others still do need the earlier conversational layer first because not enough appointments are even getting onto the calendar cleanly.
Sub: Below: where each workflow fits, how to tell which layer should come first, what proof honestly supports the comparison, and when a service business should fix booked-appointment protection before building a broader appointment-setter workflow.
