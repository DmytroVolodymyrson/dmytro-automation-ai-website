### Hero
Heading: What to Automate First for Dental Practices
Body: If your dental practice knows it needs better automation, the safest first move usually is not a giant all-in-one front-desk rebuild. It is one bounded workflow that fixes the leak already costing you bookings, chair utilization, or repeat-patient revenue. For most dental practices, that first workflow is one of five things: new-patient follow-up, appointment booking and reminders, recall and reactivation, no-show reduction, or after-hours call handling. The right choice depends on where the practice is already losing momentum today — right after the inquiry, during scheduling, on the day of the appointment, months after a patient should have come back, or after hours when nobody can answer the phone.
