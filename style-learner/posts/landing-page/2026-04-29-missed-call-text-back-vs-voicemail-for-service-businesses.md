## Final Published Text

### Hero
Heading: Missed Call Text-Back vs. Voicemail for Service Businesses
Sub: If your service business misses calls, voicemail is usually the weakest fallback because it asks the caller to do more work and wait longer. Missed-call text-back is the lighter alternative: the call is missed, an SMS goes out within seconds, and the caller gets a live next step without needing a full AI phone-answering system.

### Core sections
Heading: What this comparison is really deciding
Topics:
- Missed-call text-back keeps the lead alive
- Voicemail turns recovery into a delayed callback gamble
- Text-back is the lighter recovery layer between doing nothing and live AI phone answering
- The real issue is response lag, not whether texting sounds modern

Heading: Missed-call text-back vs. voicemail for service businesses
Topics:
- First Response: SMS within seconds vs. no response unless the caller leaves a message
- Caller Effort: business reaches back first vs. caller has to do more work and wait
- Best Fit: simple missed-call recovery vs. genuinely light call volume with disciplined callbacks
- Office Workload: text-thread follow-through vs. voicemail cleanup and urgency guesswork
- Cost Profile: low direct workflow cost vs. high hidden demand-loss cost

Heading: When each option makes sense
Topics:
- Choose missed-call text-back when the business needs something clearly better than voicemail but not every caller needs a live answer
- Keep voicemail only when call volume is truly light and callbacks are reliably fast
- Move to AI phone answering when callers regularly need live answers or after-hours demand is too valuable for delayed recovery

### FAQ
- How is this different from the broader missed-call text-back page? (This page narrows the decision to one buyer question: keep relying on voicemail after missed calls, or move to text-back first.)
- Why is missed-call text-back usually better than voicemail? (Because it responds first instead of asking the caller to do more work and wait for a callback.)
- When is voicemail still good enough? (Usually only when call volume is light, callbacks are reliably fast, and most callers do not need immediate answers.)
- When should a service business skip text-back and go straight to AI phone answering? (When callers regularly need live answers, routine intake should happen on the call itself, or after-hours demand is too valuable to leave to delayed recovery.)
- What does a focused missed-call text-back workflow usually cost? (Typically around $1.5K-$2.5K plus modest SMS usage costs.)
