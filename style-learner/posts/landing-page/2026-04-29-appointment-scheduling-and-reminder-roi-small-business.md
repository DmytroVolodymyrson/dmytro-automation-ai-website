### FAQ
Heading: How do I estimate ROI on appointment scheduling and reminder automation?
Body: Start with how many booked appointments are lost to no-shows, late cancellations, unanswered reschedules, or missing prep steps each month, and what one recovered slot is worth. Then add saved admin time from fewer manual confirmations, reminder texts, and calendar-chasing tasks.

Heading: How quickly can scheduling and reminder automation pay for itself?
Body: For many appointment-driven small businesses, payback can happen within one to three months if a few saved appointments per month already carry real value.

Heading: How is this different from the scheduling and reminder cost page?
Body: The cost page is about what the workflow costs to build and run. This page is about what the business gets back: recovered appointments, cleaner calendar utilization, saved admin time, and how many protected slots it takes for the build to pay back.

Heading: How is this different from the no-show reduction ROI page?
Body: The no-show page is narrower and focuses on attendance protection only. This page is broader inside the scheduling cluster because it also includes confirmation discipline, reminder timing, reschedule handling, prep-step enforcement, and handoff after a real appointment should already exist.

Heading: When is a manual process better than automated scheduling and reminder workflows?
Body: If appointment volume is low enough that someone can still confirm, remind, and reschedule consistently with a calendar reminder and a text thread, manual may still be enough. Automation becomes more valuable once manual follow-through keeps slipping.
