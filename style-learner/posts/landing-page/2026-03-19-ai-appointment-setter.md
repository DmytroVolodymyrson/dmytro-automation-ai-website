### What an AI appointment setter actually does
Intro: The term gets used loosely, so here is what it means in practice for a service business:
- Answers calls and messages: 24/7. Handles initial conversation, qualifying questions, basic service information.
- Books appointments directly: Checks calendar, offers slots, confirms bookings. Sends confirmations to both sides.
- Qualifies leads before booking: Asks screening questions. Routes qualified leads to booking, flags the rest.
- Sends reminders and reduces no-shows: Automated confirmation and reminder sequences. 20 to 40% no-show reduction typical.
- Follows up with leads who do not book: Automatic follow-up over next few days for leads who engage but do not complete booking.
- Hands off to your team when needed: Complex requests, complaints, or anything outside scope gets routed to a human with full context.

### Where it helps most
Six problem scenarios: Missed after-hours leads, Slow follow-up on inquiries, Staff time wasted on scheduling, High no-show rates, Inconsistent lead qualification, Seasonal volume spikes.

### Businesses that benefit most
Intro: AI appointment setters work best for businesses where revenue depends on booked appointments.
- Medical and dental practices: Patient scheduling, intake, reminders
- Home services (HVAC, plumbing, electrical): Service calls, estimates, dispatch
- Salons and spas: Booking, confirmations, waitlist management
- Legal and financial services: Consultation booking, qualification, follow-up
- Real estate: Showing scheduling, buyer/seller qualification
- Fitness and wellness: Class booking, personal training, trial sessions

### What an AI setter should not replace
Four boundaries: Complex consultations requiring empathy/negotiation, Complaint resolution, High-stakes decisions (medical/legal/financial advice), Relationship-building with key accounts.
Closing: The best results come from using AI for the repetitive 70 to 80% and freeing your team to focus on the interactions that actually require a person.

### Good fit / not a fit
Good fit: Revenue depends on appointments, losing after-hours leads, staff time on scheduling, clear booking flow, no-shows are costly.
Not a fit: Business does not rely on appointments, fewer than 10 inquiries/week, every interaction needs deep customization, no defined scheduling process, looking for a full sales closer.
