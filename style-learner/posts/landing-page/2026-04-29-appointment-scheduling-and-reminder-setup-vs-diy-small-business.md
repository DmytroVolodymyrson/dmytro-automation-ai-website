### Hero
Heading: Appointment Scheduling and Reminder Setup: Hire Help or DIY?
Body: DIY can be enough when the scheduling workflow is still narrow, the booking trigger is clean, appointment types are simple, and one person inside the business can reliably test reminder timing, reschedules, prep steps, and CRM visibility without letting edge cases leak into live customer communication. It stops being enough when reminders fire from the wrong trigger, reschedules keep the old sequence alive, prep-step failures waste higher-value appointments, or the office no longer trusts what the system says about who is actually confirmed. The real decision is not whether reminders matter. It is whether your business can still own the workflow discipline needed to make scheduling automation dependable.
Sub: Below: when DIY is still the right move, when expert setup is worth paying for, what hidden costs owners ignore when they keep patching the workflow alone, and how to decide before a broader scheduling project turns into calendar cleanup.
