### Hero
Heading: Client Reactivation ROI for Small Business
Body: Client reactivation usually pays back when the business already earned trust once, has enough usable past-client data to act on, and only needs a modest lift in repeat jobs, maintenance visits, referrals, or returning accounts to cover the build. The ROI is not magical. It comes from recovering revenue that should not have been abandoned in the first place: HVAC tune-up customers nobody checked back with, cleaning clients who lapsed quietly, deferred-work auto-repair clients who never got a reminder, and past project customers who would likely hire again if someone reached out at the right time. The wrong business will overestimate what a dormant list can do. The right business can justify a focused reactivation workflow with conservative math and one or two recovered opportunities per month.
