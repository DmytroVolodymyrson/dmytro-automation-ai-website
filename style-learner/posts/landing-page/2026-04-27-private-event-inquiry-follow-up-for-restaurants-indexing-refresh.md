Heading: Private Event Inquiry Follow-Up for Restaurants
Body: If your restaurant hosts buyouts, private dining, group bookings, or catering requests, the first operational risk is usually not demand — it is response speed. Event leads arrive through forms, voicemails, DMs, and rushed phone calls, then sit unanswered while service is happening. By the time your events lead sees the inquiry, the prospect has already reached out to another venue. Private-event inquiry follow-up automation fixes that narrow gap: it captures the key details, confirms the inquiry in under a minute, alerts the right person with a usable summary, and keeps the thread moving until a human takes over the actual sales conversation.

### New concrete workflow section
Heading: The 5-step intake-to-handoff workflow
Intro: Most restaurant event follow-up problems trace back to the same five gaps. Here is the concrete sequence automation should cover.

1. Funnel every channel into one intake record
- Normalizes phone calls, forms, email, Instagram DMs, and voicemails into one structured record.
- Explicitly names contact name, date range, estimated headcount, occasion type, and contact method.
- Ties phone-origin event leads back to the same intake path used by voice-agent or missed-call recovery.

2. Acknowledge the inquiry in under 60 seconds
- Adds a direct answer around immediate confirmation by text, email, or DM.
- Keeps the message factual: received, who owns next step, realistic follow-up window.
- Frames the main conversion leak clearly: prospects assume nobody saw the inquiry and move to another venue.

3. Alert the events lead with a usable summary
- Turns vague forwarded messages into structured notifications with inquiry source, requested date, group size, occasion, and notes.
- Keeps the human starting point stronger by avoiding repeated discovery questions.

4. Escalate or reassign if the first-response window passes
- Adds an explicit internal escalation rule if no response happens within the target window.
- Makes the workflow about service discipline, not robotic prospect messaging.

5. Follow up if the prospect goes quiet
- Uses one narrow polite follow-up plus a reminder task for the human owner.
- Explicitly avoids turning private-event sales into a spammy nurture sequence.
