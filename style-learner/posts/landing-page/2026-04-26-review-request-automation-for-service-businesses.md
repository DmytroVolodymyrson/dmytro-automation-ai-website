### Hero
Heading: Review Request Automation for Service Businesses
Body: Most service businesses do not have a review problem. They have a post-service follow-up problem. The work gets done, the customer seems happy, and then nobody asks — or the ask goes out too early, too late, or with no guardrail for unhappy replies. Review request automation fixes the narrow workflow between a completed job and a public reputation signal. It gives the business a reliable way to ask at the right time, route problems back inside before they become public complaints, and stop depending on whoever in the office remembers to send the link.
Sub: Below: what a practical review-request workflow should handle, how it stays distinct from lead follow-up and phone answering pages, when it is a good fit, what guardrails matter, and what proof honestly supports this page across the live vertical review pages already on the site.

### Core sections
Heading: What review-request automation should actually handle
Body: The page keeps the scope tightly on the post-service reputation layer: a real completed-job trigger, timing that matches the service type, different paths for satisfied customers and unhappy replies, clean field-to-office handoff, routing when a reply opens a new opportunity, and visibility into which jobs actually produce reviews.

Heading: How this page stays distinct from lead follow-up and phone answering
Body: The comparison table makes the workflow boundaries explicit. AI lead follow-up, instant response, contact-form recovery, and quote follow-up all cover earlier stages before or around booking. This page isolates only the post-job reputation layer after the work is complete.

Heading: Is this a good fit for your business?
Body: The fit framing stays practical. This workflow is strongest when the team completes enough jobs that review consistency materially affects trust, map-pack visibility, or referrals, but the review ask still depends on memory. It is not the right fit when the bigger leak is still missed calls, slow lead response, low completed-job volume, or unstable service recovery.

Heading: Guardrails that keep review-request automation useful
Body: The page stresses not automating around unresolved service issues, respecting the difference between quick jobs and longer engagements, keeping the field-to-office signal clean, and measuring review quality instead of only message volume.

Heading: How a practical review-request workflow usually works
Body: The flow stays simple and stage-aware: a reliable completion signal starts the closeout stage, some service types use a soft check-in first, satisfied replies move toward the public review ask, uncertain replies route back inside fast, and owners can finally see where post-job reputation follow-through breaks.
