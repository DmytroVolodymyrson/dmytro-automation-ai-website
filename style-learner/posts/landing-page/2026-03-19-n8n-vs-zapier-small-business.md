### The short version
Intro: If you need a quick decision framework, here it is:
Zapier is probably enough if:
- You need simple triggers: when X happens, do Y
- You use mainstream SaaS tools (Gmail, Slack, HubSpot, Sheets)
- You want zero setup and zero maintenance
- Your automations are under 5 steps each
- You are not technical and do not have a developer on call
n8n is the better fit if:
- Your workflows have branching logic, loops, or error handling
- You need to connect to APIs that Zapier does not support natively
- You want to own your data and not depend on a third-party cloud
- Your Zapier bill is climbing past $100/month
- You have a developer or consultant who can build and maintain it

### What each tool actually is
Zapier: Cloud-only. No-code. Connects 6,000+ apps with a trigger-action model. Optimized for speed-to-value.
n8n: Open-source. Visual workflow builder with a code option. Can be self-hosted or used via n8n Cloud. Optimized for flexibility.

### The cost question
Three cards:
- Zapier costs scale with volume: Per-task pricing. A 5-step workflow processing 1,000 records/month uses 5,000 tasks. Monthly bills can reach $200 to $800+ at higher volumes.
- n8n costs scale with complexity: Cloud charges by workflow count, not task volume. Self-hosted costs $5 to $50/month for server. Setup and maintenance take more effort.
- The hidden cost is time: Zapier saves time upfront but costs more at scale. n8n saves money at scale but costs more time upfront. Break-even is around $100 to $150/month in Zapier spend.

### When this decision actually matters
Four scenarios: High-volume lead processing, Complex conditional logic, Custom API connections, Data privacy requirements.
Closing: If none of these apply and your automations are straightforward, Zapier is a fine choice. Do not over-engineer the decision.

### Who should use which
Stick with Zapier: Non-technical, simple workflows, popular SaaS tools, manageable task volume, no data residency needs.
Move to n8n: Zapier bill over $100/month, need conditions/loops/error handling, custom APIs, want to own infrastructure, have dev/consultant available.
