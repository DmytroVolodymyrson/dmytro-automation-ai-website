Heading: Estimate Follow-Up ROI for Small Business
Body: If your business sends estimates after site visits and a meaningful share of them go cold, estimate-follow-up automation can have short payback. The ROI does not come from AI hype — it comes from recovering jobs that already made it past the inspection. You paid for the truck roll, the estimator's time, and the office work to prepare the quote. When that estimate dies quietly because nobody followed up at day 3 or day 7, the sunk cost is real and the lost revenue is measurable. The useful question is not whether automation sounds good in theory. It is whether the math works with conservative assumptions: a modest number of recovered estimates per month, some saved admin time, and better visibility into where open quotes are stalling.
